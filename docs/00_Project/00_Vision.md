# 1. Purpose

This document defines the long-term vision of the Heroes Online project.

It describes why the game exists, what experience it should provide, what principles guide development, and how every future design and technical decision must be evaluated.

This document is the highest-level reference for the entire project.

If any future document contradicts this Vision, the contradiction must be resolved before implementation.

---

# 2. Project Mission

Heroes Online is a persistent massively multiplayer online strategy game that rewards planning, cooperation, and long-term progression.

The game is designed to create stories driven by players rather than scripted content.

Our goal is to build a living world where thousands of players simultaneously develop cities, expand territories, cooperate with allies, compete against rivals, and influence the history of the world.

---

# 3. Vision Statement

Heroes Online will become a modern browser-first MMO strategy game combining:

- deep city management;
- meaningful strategic decisions;
- large-scale alliance warfare;
- long-term character progression;
- fair competitive gameplay;
- stable technical architecture;
- continuous content expansion.

The project is designed to evolve for many years without requiring architectural redesign.

---

# 4. Core Principles

Every system developed for Heroes Online must follow these principles.

## 4.1 Strategy over Speed

Victory should primarily depend on planning and decision making rather than constant online presence.

---

## 4.2 Long-Term Progression

Player progress must remain meaningful over months and years.

Development should feel rewarding without requiring endless repetition.

---

## 4.3 Meaningful Choices

Every important decision should involve trade-offs.

There should rarely be a single objectively correct choice.

---

## 4.4 Social Gameplay

Players should naturally benefit from cooperation.

Alliances are expected to become one of the main gameplay pillars.

---

## 4.5 Persistent World

The world continues to exist regardless of whether individual players are online.

Time never stops.

---

## 4.6 Server Authority

The server is the only source of truth.

Clients never decide game outcomes.

---

## 4.7 Expandability

Every game system must be designed for future expansion.

New mechanics should integrate into existing architecture without major rewrites.

---

The player should experience the following journey.

### Early Game

Learn.

Build.

Explore.

Understand.

Feel constant progression.

---

### Mid Game

Optimize.

Expand.

Join alliances.

Develop specialized strategies.

Participate in diplomacy.

---

### Late Game

Lead.

Conquer.

Coordinate alliances.

Compete for world dominance.

Create memorable stories.

---

# 6. What Heroes Online Is

Heroes Online is:

- a real-time MMO strategy game;
- a city-building game;
- a grand strategy game;
- a multiplayer social experience;
- a long-term progression game;
- a continuously evolving online service.

---

# 7. What Heroes Online Is Not

Heroes Online is not:

- an idle clicker;
- a pay-to-win product;
- an action game;
- a single-player RPG;
- a short-session casual game;
- a copy of any existing title.

---

# 8. Target Audience

Primary audience:

- players aged approximately 18–45;
- fans of MMO strategy games;
- players who enjoy long-term progression;
- competitive and cooperative players;
- browser and cross-platform users.

No geographic region is considered primary. The project is intended for an international audience from the beginning.

---

# 9. Business Philosophy

Heroes Online is developed as a long-term live service.

Revenue should come from optional convenience and cosmetic features rather than mandatory purchases required to remain competitive.

Fair competition has higher priority than short-term monetization.

---

# 10. Technical Philosophy

The project is built using modern engineering practices.

Every feature should be:

- documented;
- testable;
- maintainable;
- scalable;
- observable;
- secure.

Technical debt should be minimized whenever practical.

---

# 11. Definition of Success

The project is considered successful if:

- players remain active for years;
- alliances create their own stories;
- new content can be added without redesigning the architecture;
- the game remains technically stable under high load;
- gameplay rewards strategy more than spending.

---

# 12. Long-Term Vision

Heroes Online is designed as a platform rather than a finished product.

Future expansions may introduce:

- additional continents;
- new civilizations;
- seasonal worlds;
- cooperative PvE events;
- world bosses;
- naval gameplay;
- advanced diplomacy;
- guild technologies;
- player-created tournaments.

These additions must extend the existing architecture rather than replace it.

---

# 13. Vision Validation

Every future design proposal should answer the following questions:

1. Does this improve strategic gameplay?
2. Does this create meaningful choices?
3. Does this support long-term progression?
4. Does this encourage social interaction?
5. Can this scale technically?
6. Can this be maintained for many years?

If the answer to any question is "No", the proposal must be reconsidered.

---

# 14. Approval

This document establishes the strategic direction of the Heroes Online project.

All future documentation, design decisions, and implementation work shall follow this vision.
